Excited to announce that we got backed by Founders, Inc.

Last month, we applied to f.inc artifact without a real product. We didn't expect to get in, but we thought it was worth a shot.

Then something unexpected happened. Hubert Thieblot didn't care about the product. He bet on our potential. We got in, and booked our flight from Korea to San Francisco the next day.

But there still was a problem. We were supposed to be in San Francisco the following week, and we had nothing to show.

We had one idea though "What if you could talk to an AI avatar of Steve Jobs and get real business advice?" We built a fully interactive, conversational avatar in 2 days and shipped it. 

Things got crazy. It spread on Twitter. 10,000 users in three weeks.

Investors and founders reached out. Half of the people of Artifact already knew us because they saw us on twitter.

But we wanted to do something even bigger. Our team always look for momentum.

Around that time, OpenClaw happened. We thought that the most interesting part of openclaw is that it communicates across every channel the user use, while holding the full context of every past interaction. It feels like a real person.

We asked ourselves: how do we maximize the feeling that an openclaw agent is a real person?

The answer was Clawra. The world's first openclaw agent with its own face and its own personality. 

We built it in a day and launched it.

What happened next surprised us. 1M impressions, 10k users, and 1k GitHub stars in a single day. The product was picked up by This Week in Startups, shared by top influencers on tiktok/instagram, and covered in press across the US, Europe, and Asia.

Founders Inc invested. Other VCs followed.

The AI companion and assistant market is one of the largest opportunities in technology. The race has just started, and right now, we're ahead. When people think “openclaw companion/assistant,” they think Clawra.

Our first 10,000 users came in a single day. But almost all of them were developers, maybe 1% of the world. The other 99%, the people who have never touched a terminal, want a Samantha (from the movie "Her") too. We're building Clawra for them now. 

We ship fast, collect feedback fast, and grow fast.

We're just getting started. And we're winning.… more
