About
Asim Ahmed is a multi-award-winning game developer and head of product marketing at Niantic Spatial, Inc., the leader in geospatial AI. 

Prior to Niantic Spatial, he spent close to a decade at Niantic, Inc. where he was one of the company's earliest marketing hires. During his time at Niantic he played a pivotal role in launching Pokémon GO to over 1 billion users and was also instrumental in a variety of other projects, collaborating with some of the world’s most iconic intellectual properties such as Harry Potter and Monster Hunter. He also was a critical member in developing a brand new IP with the Peridot franchise, shipping across multiple platforms including mobile, mixed-reality headsets, and AR glasses, working alongside major partners such as Snap and Meta.

Driven by a passion for pushing the boundaries of technology, Asim thrives on exploring new frontiers in augmented reality, artificial intelligence, spatial computing, and wearable technologies.

Asim’s time with Niantic in a nutshell:

Currently at Niantic Spatial, Inc.
- Head of Product Marketing, focused on the Peridot franchise, other immersive entertainment projects, and platform products

Previously at Niantic, Inc. 
- Launched and supported the global gaming phenomenon Pokémon GO to 1B+ users, including co-creating Community Day and the initial Pokémon GO Fest
- Launched and eventually led marketing for Harry Potter: Wizards Unite
- Launched and led marketing for our AR-first, first party original franchise Peridot across mobile, mixed-reality headsets, and AR glasses platforms
- Supported numerous Ingress and Niantic Brand marketing initiatives
- Advised and contributed to a variety of other early development, soft launch, and global launch Niantic titles, including Pikmin Bloom and Monster Hunter Now

Niantic, Inc. spun out of Google as an independent company in late 2015, focused on building planet-scale AR experiences for the real world with the mission of inspiring exploration, encouraging exercise, and creating connection. Niantic, Inc.'s games business was acquired by Scopely in 2025 for $3.85B. Concurrently, a new spin-out, Niantic Spatial, Inc., was formed to focus on building a leading geospatial AI model and spatial intelligence platform to understand and interact with the world.
