The AI chat window is dead.

I’m sitting at Perplexity AI’s conference right now watching them announce Perplexity Computer. Mobile, Enterprise, Personal. And it’s one of those moments where you can feel the ground shifting under everything.
AI isn’t answering your questions anymore. It’s operating your computer. Opening apps, navigating UIs, completing tasks end to end, on your behalf. The interface is just… gone. Or at least it’s becoming irrelevant.

We’ve been talking about “agentic AI” for a couple years now but this is what it actually looks like when it ships. Not a demo. Not a research paper. A product you can use today.
Think about what that means for how people work. The unit of AI value stops being “a good answer” and starts being “a completed task.” That’s a fundamentally different thing.
I’ve spent 13+ years watching interfaces evolve. Physical to touchscreen, touchscreen to voice, voice to AR. Every time a new interface layer collapses, the stuff built on top of the old one has to rethink itself from scratch.

We’re in that moment right now. Except the interface that’s collapsing is… everything.
The question I keep coming back to is not “can AI do this” (it clearly can). It’s what gets rebuilt when the assumption of “a human will click the button” is no longer a given.

Wild time to be paying attention.… more
