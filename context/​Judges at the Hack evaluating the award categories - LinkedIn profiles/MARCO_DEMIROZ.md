CEO at Resolve | Helping construction go faster by finding issues early and closing the design-delivery gap

Visit my website

5d • 


Over the past few weeks the Resolve team has been improving our internal tooling to take full advantage of tools like Claude Code to accelerate our product development. One of the Claude skills that my dev friends keep recommending is Compound Engineering. And it's a concept that we want to bring to construction.

From the developers of Compound Engineering:
“In compound engineering, you expect each feature to make the next feature easier to build. This is because compound engineering creates a learning loop for your agents and members of your team, so that each bug, failed test, or a-ha problem-solving insight gets documented and used by future agents.”

The idea is that development becomes a learning loop where every improvement teaches the system how to move faster next time.

An example of what this looks like in practice: 

If Claude Code fixes a bug, you don’t just merge the fix.
You also update the rules and documentation the next agent will read so that this class of bug doesn’t happen again.
So over time, the system gets smarter about building itself.

This is how the Resolve team thinks construction should work. 

I’ve seen the same data center design built multiple times, but even when the design is the same, projects still repeat mistakes, reinvent workflows, and adapt in ways that undo the benefits of standardization

Lessons disappear into meetings, emails, or someone’s memory. And in an industry where over 20% of the workforce is over 55 years of age, decades of construction intelligence are quietly retiring every year.

Compound engineering in construction starts when we:
• Digitize workflows
• Bring domain experts together with AI
• Turn reviews and coordination into systems that remember

When that happens, every project doesn’t just finish.
It teaches the next project how to move faster.… more
